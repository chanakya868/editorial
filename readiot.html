IoT in Gaming Industry

“With the IoT, we are headed to a world where things aren’t liable to break catastrophically or at least we’ll have a hell of heads’ up. We're headed to a world where our doors unlock when they sense us nearby.”

The Internet of things (IoT) has its applications in vivid fields, name a field and you can see it growing with the help of IoT.

What is IoT?

In a nutshell, the Internet of Things is a system of pertinent computing devices, mechanical and digital machines, objects, animals or people that are provided with unique identifiers and the ability to transfer data over a colossal network of connecting things and people – all of which collect and share data about the way they are used and about the environment around them.

A forementioned includes a phenomenal number of objects of all shapes and sizes – from microwaves which automatically cook your food for the right amount of time, to self-driving cars whose complex sensors detect an object in their path, to wearable fitness devices that measure your heart rate and the number of steps you have taken that day, to fuel technological innovation in gaming that has changed the way we play by making games more interactive.

How can you use IoT in the gaming industry?

IoT brought stir in the gaming industry. It promises great benefits for many businesses and the gaming industry is one of them. Evolving every year at an increasing note, the industry causes the companies to search for new solutions to stand out. By implementing IoT in video games, the whole market can be changed once and for all.

IoT in the mobile gaming industry:

Despite all the top-rated games being made for powerful consoles like Playstation4, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch and personal computers, the most widespread gaming platform that takes almost half of the global market is the mobile phone.

The mobile gaming industry, just as IoT, has incredible growth in the last few years. According to recent research, it takes a significant 47% of the global gaming market. The mobile gaming business is estimated to be worth $62.3 billion and will continue to grow as 20192.4 billion people will be playing mobile games in the next coming years. With such a high stride of evolution, mobile gaming requires new technological approaches, and IoT suits perfectly.

One of the best examples is a game that was released last year, Pokemon Go. By combining both AR and IoT technology Nintendo’s game made the whole world go around looking for magical creatures to catch. The game launched in June 2016 and achieved a record of $207 million in its first month.

The reason for such triumph of Pokémon Go is in the experience the player gets because of the mix of AR and IoT. Every person takes the role of a trainer whose goal is to catch as many Pokémon as possible. By walking around in real life, interacting with other players and objects, the player gets more engaged in the game, as it becomes a part of the real world. This is one of the most important things IoT can bring into the mobile gaming industry – the game becomes interesting with broader smartphone screens and involves the player into a real-life experience. By connecting other objects to the game, a new gaming experience emerges and this is the thing industry needs today.

IoT in educational games:

Making education exciting, a “Nah” would be the expression, but IoT proved it wrong by bringing in a lot of exciting games into the picture like a treasure hunt. IoT took a quantum leap by entering into the field of educational games. Games captivate the attention of players and keep them focused on the task in hand. Their fun character grabs their attention and intrigues them to complete the games. Students are the lead players to complete goals that have been set while teachers can generate feedback and track their progress. Of course, not anymore, not every game can be an educational game, even if it possesses educational content. The key is design. It is necessary to design in such a way that it will drive the players to immerse themselves and test their knowledge to their fullest capabilities.

All these technologies are helping teachers to create ubiquitous educational environments and to adapt them to characteristics of the educational process or students’ needs.

IoT in the mobile casino:

Many of the popular online games today come under the gambling genre. The casino industry tends to use modern technologies to engage new customers by shifting the platform from the real table into the web. By doing so, every person can play from his/her mobile device whenever they want to and therefore be a part of the game more often. IoT can bring the experience of physical gaming into the online casino business.

IoT and physical gaming sensors:

Another great example of connecting the real-life experience with the virtual world is the gaming sensor costumes. If the IoT controllers change the way players interact with the game, the sensor suite lets the players feel the game on themselves. Every hit, every shot, and every fall from the skyscraper, players experience everything that their character does. IoT does everything to woo us from the basic casino playing to jumping from skyscrapers, you can feel everything sitting at a place. The gaming community is very enthusiastic and ready to get a higher score in terms of everything. That means using new technologies to get exciting experience as well. And this is where IoT gets into the picture. In this age of the fourth Industrial Revolution, things are changing rapidly and this is further fuelled by the advent of IoT.
