Automatic Transformer Cooling Using SCADA

A transformer is a static device, which transfers the power from one circuit to another circuit without a change in frequency. These transformers play a vital role in the transmission and distribution of power according to the consumer's needs. Under heavy load conditions, the transformer's temperature rises due to the power loss dissipated by the transformer in the form of heat.

To maintain the transformer’s temperature moderately (40°c to 45°c), cooling systems are introduced for about 25kVA transformers.

But these cooling systems can only be activated manually after checking the temperature of the transformer by the operator.

Due to this manual operation, there may be chances of damage to the transformer due to the negligence of work (Human Error). A transformer operating at just 10° above its rating will reduce its life by 50%.

So, to employ a transformer with a cooling system that could activate within the meantime of the rise in the transformer temperature automatically and can deactivate when the transformer gets to normal temperature, we can use a temperature sensor LM35(-55°C to 150°C).

LM35 is a temperature measuring device having an analog output voltage proportional to the temperature. It provides output voltage in Centigrade (Celsius). It does not require any external calibration circuitry. The sensitivity of LM35 is 10 mV/degree Celsius. As the temperature increases, the output voltage also increases.

If we install the temperature sensor near the transformer, whenever there is a rise in temperature beyond a predetermined value, it cools the transformer temperature. Also, we can check the temperature of the transformer in a digital format by installing an LCD with the temperature sensor circuit.

Conclusion:

1) This could efface Human errors.
2) The speed of operation is quick compared to manual operation.
